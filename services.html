Common Issues

We Solve for Houston Homeowners
Roofing Problems

01
Climate Damage

Sun & Heat Damage

Houston's intense UV exposure and heat cycles cause shingles to lose protective granules, crack, and curl faster than in most climates. South and west-facing slopes are especially vulnerable. We install UV-resistant architectural shingles rated for Texas heat and provide honest assessments on when repairs make sense versus full replacement.

02
Weather Events

Storm & Wind Damage

Gulf Coast storms bring high winds that lift shingles, tear flashing, and drive rain into vulnerable areas. Hail cracks shingles and damages roof penetrations. Most storm damage isn't visible from the ground—you need a professional inspection to catch it before the next storm causes interior leaks.

03
Hidden Leaks

Flashing Failures

Small leaks around chimneys, skylights, or roof valleys often go unnoticed until water stains appear on ceilings. By then, the underlying roof deck may have significant rot requiring structural repair. We catch leaks early through regular inspections and fix flashing before it becomes a costly issue.

04
Moisture Issues

Algae & Moss Growth

Algae, moss, and mold thrive in Houston's humid climate, especially on shaded north-facing slopes. Algae trap moisture and accelerate shingle decay. Moss roots burrow under shingles and lift them, creating leak points. We provide professional cleaning, zinc strip installation, and shingle replacement where needed.

FAQ
How much does a roof replacement cost in Houston?
Most residential roof replacements cost between $8,000 and $15,000 in Houston, depending on roof size, pitch, material choice, and complexity. A simple single-story home with basic architectural shingles will be on the lower end. A two-story home with steep pitches, multiple valleys, or premium shingles will cost more. We provide detailed written estimates with material options so you understand exactly what you're paying for.
What roofing materials do you recommend?
Asphalt architectural shingles are the most popular choice for Houston roofs—they offer excellent wind resistance (up to 130 mph ratings), good warranties (25–50 years), and a wide range of colors and styles. For flat or low-slope roofs, TPO (thermoplastic polyolefin) is a durable, energy-efficient option. Metal roofing is also gaining popularity for its longevity and energy savings, though it costs more upfront. We'll walk you through material options, pros and cons, and costs during your free estimate.
Should I repair or replace my roof?
That depends on the age and condition of your roof. If your roof is under 15 years old with isolated damage (a few missing shingles, one small leak, minor flashing damage), a repair is usually the right call. If your roof is over 20 years old, has multiple leaks, widespread shingle damage, or visible sagging, replacement is often more cost-effective long-term. We'll give you an honest assessment during your free inspection—if a repair will solve the problem, we'll tell you.
