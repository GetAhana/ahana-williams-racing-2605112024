Houston Roofing Challenges

Houston's climate is hard on roofs—intense sun, heavy rain, high winds, and humidity create problems most homeowners don't see until it's too late. Williams Roofing has seen them all, and we know how to fix them right the first time.

01
Climate Wear

Sun & Heat Damage

Houston's UV exposure breaks down shingle protective layers faster than in most climates. Shingles lose granules, crack, curl, and fail prematurely—especially on south and west-facing slopes. We install UV-resistant shingles rated for Texas heat and provide honest assessments on when repairs make sense versus replacement.

02
Weather Events

Storm & Wind Damage

Gulf Coast storms bring high winds that lift shingles, tear flashing, and drive rain into vulnerable areas. Hail cracks shingles and damages vents. After severe weather, most damage isn't visible from the ground—you need a professional inspection to catch it before the next storm causes leaks.

03
Hidden Leaks

Flashing Failures

Small leaks around chimneys, skylights, or valleys often go unnoticed until water stains appear on ceilings. By then, the underlying roof deck may have significant rot requiring structural repair. We catch leaks early through regular inspections and fix flashing before it becomes a costly interior damage issue.

04
Moisture Issues

Algae & Moss Growth

Algae, moss, and mold thrive in Houston's humidity, especially on shaded north-facing slopes. Algae trap moisture and accelerate shingle decay. Moss roots burrow under shingles and lift them, creating leak points. We provide professional cleaning, zinc strip installation, and shingle replacement where needed.

Roofing Questions

Common questions Houston homeowners ask about roof replacement, repair, and maintenance—answered by Williams Roofing's 15+ years of hands-on experience.

How long does a roof replacement take?
Most residential roof replacements take 1–3 days depending on roof size, complexity, and weather. Simple single-story homes may be completed in one day. Larger homes with steep pitches, multiple valleys, or extensive chimney/skylight work take 2–3 days. We'll give you an accurate timeline during your free estimate.
Should I repair or replace my roof?
That depends on the age and condition of your current roof. If your roof is under 15 years old with isolated damage (a few missing shingles, one leak), a repair is usually the right choice. If your roof is over 20 years old, has widespread damage, multiple leaks, or shows heavy granule loss, replacement is often more cost-effective long-term. We'll give you an honest assessment during your free inspection.
What roofing materials do you recommend for Houston?
Asphalt shingles are the most common choice for Houston homes—affordable, durable, and available in many styles. Architectural shingles (also called dimensional shingles) offer better wind resistance and longer warranties than 3-tab shingles. For flat or low-slope roofs, TPO or modified bitumen are popular choices. We'll walk you through material options, warranties, and costs during your estimate.
Do you offer a warranty on your work?
Yes. Every Williams Roofing installation includes a 1-year workmanship warranty covering materials and installation quality. We also help you register manufacturer warranties, which typically range from 25 to 50 years depending on the shingle grade you select. If any issues arise due to our work, we'll return to fix them at no charge.
